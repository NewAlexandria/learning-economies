# Mohawk College - Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) Research Centre

## Program Overview
- **Institution**: Mohawk College
- **Program**: Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) Research Centre
- **Type**: Applied research and development facility (not a traditional academic program)
- **URL**: https://ideaworks.mohawkcollege.ca/research-centre/industrial-internet-of-things-iiot/

## Research Focus Areas

### Core Expertise
- **Wireless Sensor Devices**: For manufacturing and industrial applications
- **Energy Harvesting**: Solutions to extend battery life
- **Digital Twin Technology**: Virtual representations of physical systems
- **Configurable Sensors**: Customizable sensor solutions
- **Robotics Integration**: IoT applications in robotics
- **AI Integration**: Leveraging artificial intelligence in industrial IoT

## Services Offered

### 1. Product Development and Improvement
- Collaboration with industry experts
- Prototyping and product enhancement
- Leveraging sensor technology for product innovation
- Proof-of-concept development
- Prototype system development

### 2. Process Improvement
- Applied research for operational efficiency
- Tailored solutions for SMEs (Small and Medium Enterprises)
- Process optimization through IoT implementation
- Innovation enhancement
- Seamless integration support
- Measurable results tracking

## Target Audience
- Canadian companies looking to adopt Industrial IoT solutions
- Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs)
- Industry partners seeking applied research collaboration

## Notes
- This is primarily a research centre rather than a structured academic program
- Focuses on applied research and industry collaboration
- Does not offer a traditional curriculum like degree programs
- Services are tailored to specific industry needs
- Relevant for OT security as it deals with industrial IoT systems

## OT Security Relevance
While not a formal OT security training program, the IIoT Research Centre's work with industrial control systems, IoT devices, and critical infrastructure makes it relevant to OT security research and development.
